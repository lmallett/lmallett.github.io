Solar Physics

Most recent poster, presented at the Coronal Loops Workshop in Tenerife, Spain in 2024.

In the summer of 2023, I attended the University of Alabama in Huntsville and NASA MSFC Heliophysics REU, where I joined a project with Dr. Amy Winebarger and Dr. P.S. Athiray.

This project concerns differential emission measure (DEM) inversion. DEM inversion is a technique used to derive thermal distributions of plasma along line-of-sight using images of the Sun in multiple wavelengths.

Specifically, the project concerns areas of the Sun where DEM inversion fails. It turns out that DEM inversion consistently has no solution at the tops of loops during and after solar flares.

Particle Cosmology

Poster presented to Harvey Mudd College student research symposium.

I worked with professor Brian Shuve at Harvey Mudd College from the fall of 2021 through the summer of 2022. Over the school year, this was for credit; during the summer it became a full-time paid position.

This project developed a model to explain matter-antimatter asymmetry and the neutrino mass problem simultaneously via the introduction of a scalar boson and sterile neutrinos. Prof. Shuve goes further in-depth here.

My main tasks for the project were:

  • implementing a set of coupled partial differential equations in Mathematica, and numerically solving them to investigate if the model could reproduce observed values for matter-antimatter asymmetry in the Universe, and
  • comparing this implementation to two other implementations of the same problem, and correcting errors in all three. This involved in-depth debugging, as well as deriving equations using careful calculus and linear algebra.

This work is related to arXiv:1704.01979. Though ultimately the model I worked with did not produce fruitful results, I gained valuable skills throughout the experience.
